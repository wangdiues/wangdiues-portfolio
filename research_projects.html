Research & Projects

Spatial Distribution and Climate Resilience of Abies densa

Developed high-resolution habitat suitability models for Abies densa using species occurrence and climate projections. Future projections revealed a contraction of suitable habitats under SSP scenarios.

  • MaxEnt modeling with future climate scenarios
  • Priority conservation zones identified

Spatial Ecology and Climate Niche Modeling of Quercus Species

Mapped distributions of dominant Quercus species across Bhutan’s elevational gradients. Richness hotspots were found between 2000–3200 m, driven by temperature and precipitation gradients.

  • MaxEnt modeling with high AUC (>0.91)
  • Kernel Density Estimation for diversity hotspots
  • Climate-driven species zonation

Forest Dynamics and Vegetation Structure in Unmanaged Forests

Surveyed vegetation patterns and classified forests into Subtropical, Warm Broadleaved, and Cool Broadleaved types based on elevation gradients. Conducted Canonical Correspondence Analysis to link species composition with environmental factors.

  • 170 tree species, 140 shrubs, 204 ground vegetation species
  • CCA: Elevation, temperature, and evapotranspiration were major drivers

Wildlife Use of Natural Salt Licks

Analyzed the chemical properties of natural salt licks and documented wildlife usage through camera traps. Barking Deer, Assamese Macaque, and Himalayan Serow were among the most frequent visitors.

  • 10 natural salt lick sites monitored
  • 300 camera trap days and 1400+ animal photos
